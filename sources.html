It also features several primary pictures and documents. The source will be used in showing the causes and effects, specifically effects, that the Christmas Truce had.

  • Janssen, Volker, and A.J Baime. "What Happened When WWI Paused for Christmas." History.com, 7 June 2019, www.history.com/topics/christmas-truce-1914-world-war-i-soldier-accounts. Accessed 4 Oct. 2021.
    This source was linked from the Christmas Truce of 1914 webpage. The source gives a more in depth look at what actually happened. It includes primary sources, first hand accounts, and military explanations. It will be used to forge the main body of my project.

  • "London News Christmas Truce." WIkipedia, en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christmas_truce#/media/File:Illustrated_London_News_-_Christmas_Truce_1914.jpg. Accessed 4 Oct. 2021. Accessed 4 Oct. 2021.
    Another great primary source, this source was an illustration of the events that took place during the Christmas Truce. The original illustration was published in a newspaper all across London on Jan 9th 1915, a few days after the Christmas Truce. The illustration features British and German soldiers meeting in "No Man's Land" to celebrate Christmas. The source will be included as a primary source in the project and used to represent how Christmas brought peace to soldiers mid war for a short time.
